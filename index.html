    Core services

    Owner-side consulting across CAPEX, procurement, commissioning and operational value.

    We support industrial CAPEX and EPC projects from early project definition to controlled execution. Our role is to protect the owner’s scope, budget, schedule and contract position by building a clear governance system around project scope, interfaces, cost control, schedule logic, risks, change orders, contractor coordination and decision-making.

    The support can include project execution strategy, scope and battery-limit review, integrated schedule assessment, CAPEX baseline check, project controls setup, interface management, contractor progress review, risk and issue management, change order control and owner-side reporting.

    The objective is to help the client keep the project under control before deviations become expensive with clear responsibilities, measurable progress, transparent risks and disciplined execution governance.

      We help investors and industrial owners validate project opportunities before committing major CAPEX. Our work connects technology selection, capacity scenarios, CAPEX / OPEX / TCO, raw material assumptions, product economics, execution risks and operational readiness into one investment decision logic.

      The goal is to move from a high-level business idea to a structured, risk-adjusted investment view: what should be built, at what scale, with which technology, under which assumptions, and with what expected commercial return.

        We provide independent technical and commercial evaluation of supplier, technology and EPC proposals before the client commits to supplier selection, contract award or major CAPEX decisions.

        The review focuses on scope completeness, technical compliance, hidden exclusions, battery limits, interface risks, utility assumptions, performance guarantees, delivery terms, commissioning obligations, acceptance criteria, warranty conditions, spare parts, lifecycle cost and total cost of ownership.

        The objective is to move procurement decisions from price comparison to risk-adjusted value comparison — helping the owner understand what is included, what is missing, what can create future cost exposure and which supplier offer provides the strongest technical, commercial and operational position.

          We perform technical and operational audits of existing crushing, refining, biodiesel and related process plants to identify hidden losses, bottlenecks, process inefficiencies and cost reduction opportunities.

          The review covers production performance, yield, energy and utilities, downtime, process stability, maintenance interaction, product quality, operating discipline and key cost drivers. Each finding is converted into a practical improvement action with expected operational and financial impact.

          The objective is not only to describe plant issues, but to build a clear performance improvement roadmap: what should be fixed first, what business impact it can generate, which actions require CAPEX, and which improvements can be achieved through process, maintenance or operational discipline.

            We help operating plants build a structured downtime control and bad actor analysis system that shows where capacity, margin and EBITDA are being lost.

            The system classifies planned and unplanned downtime, identifies recurring equipment and process issues, ranks bad actors by business impact and converts production losses into a prioritized maintenance and improvement action plan.

            This creates the data foundation for predictive maintenance: failure trend visibility, recurring issue tracking, early warning signals, maintenance priority logic and better decisions before critical assets create major production losses.

            We support the transition from construction to stable operation through commissioning planning, readiness assessment, punch list control, start-up coordination and ramp-up stabilization.

            Our focus is not only to start the equipment, but to help the plant reach agreed quantitative and qualitative performance targets: throughput, yield, product quality, utility consumption, process stability, safety readiness and reliable shift operation.

            When required, we also support operator and shift-team training, SOP preparation, start-up checklists, performance test readiness, acceptance criteria and handover to the operating team.

            Commercial-production analytics that convert plant performance into financial impact. We build crushing margin, refinery premium, gross margin, EBITDA and yield economics models, including reusable calculators and production-reporting tools for continuous management use.

              We support biodiesel and biodiesel-feedstock projects from the early business idea to technical and commercial validation.

              Our work may include feedstock market analysis, feedstock quality assessment, process route evaluation, technology selection, supplier proposal review, CAPEX / OPEX assumptions, integration with existing crushing or refining assets and development of the commercial business model.

              The goal is to help clients understand whether the biodiesel project is technically feasible, commercially attractive and operationally realistic before committing major investment.
